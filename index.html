Exploration geology and project development

Earth Source Limited advances a small portfolio of exploration projects across the UK and Ireland. The emphasis is on clear geological models, disciplined datasets, and practical field programmes that reduce uncertainty step by step.

Focus

What we do

We work from early-stage targeting through to drill planning: integrating public and historical datasets, collecting new field observations, and testing specific hypotheses with the right level of work.

  • Target generation and ranking
  • Geological mapping and sampling programmes
  • Geochemical interpretation and GIS workflows
  • Geophysics integration where relevant
  • Drill concept development and programme planning
Working principle
The story stays grounded: what is known, what is inferred, what would falsify the model, and what the next programme should aim to prove or disprove.

Projects

Irish Zinc — Duncormick

Zinc project supported by historical work and a strong dataset base. Current effort is focused on reinterpretation, target ranking, and defining the most efficient follow-up work.

  • Data compilation and reinterpretation
  • Field validation and focused sampling
  • Targets defined as testable drill concepts

Irish Lithium — Kildare & Wicklow

Greenfield lithium exploration across prospective ground. The work is staged: regional screening first, then detailed follow-up only where the geology supports it.

  • Structured screening and ranking
  • Mapping and selective sampling
  • Practical follow-up programmes

Scottish Copper

Appraisal of a historic copper district. The objective is to move from “mining heritage” to clear modern targets using a systematic approach.

  • Historic synthesis + modern datasets
  • Target ranking with explicit criteria
  • Work programmes designed around decision points

Exploration services

Where appropriate, we also provide technical support to partners: GIS, field sampling design, target generation, and project review.

  • Prospecting, mapping, sampling
  • Geochemistry workflows and interpretation
  • GIS deliverables for decision-making

Method

1) Model

Start with a clear geological model and the minimum set of observations needed to test it.

2) Data

Compile what exists, understand its limitations, then collect new data designed to close specific gaps.

3) Field programme

Execute practical mapping and sampling that supports interpretation rather than noise.

4) Decisions

Define the next step as a decision: proceed, refine, or stop — based on evidence.
